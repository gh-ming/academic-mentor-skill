# Chinese Doctoral Proposal Writing

Use this reference when drafting or revising a Chinese doctoral opening report, thesis chapter, or proposal section.

## Positioning

The deliverable must read like a real Chinese degree-thesis document rather than:

- a literature note
- a project memo
- a brainstorming draft
- a polished but generic assistant output

## Core Writing Rule

Write on one problem-driven line.

Do not try to cover every aspect evenly. Instead:

1. let heavy paragraphs carry the main argument
2. let light paragraphs handle transition or closure
3. give each paragraph one clear role
4. make the last sentence naturally lead to the next paragraph

## Background And Significance

`选题背景及意义` should usually follow this chain:

1. why the larger problem matters
2. why that problem generates a need for a certain kind of spatial information
3. why the chosen task is the key data-producing link
4. why traditional means are insufficient
5. why the proposed sensing or modeling route becomes necessary

Do not turn this section into:

- a policy list
- an application list
- a method teaser
- a mini related-work section

## Citation Surface Rule

In background-and-significance writing, citations should usually stay in parentheses and support the claim rather than interrupt the prose.

Avoid review-style surface phrasing such as:

- “某某指出”
- “某某进一步表明”
- “研究表明这一结构值得借鉴”

Prefer:

- direct judgment in the sentence
- citation in parentheses
- one or two representative sources when the claim is narrow

## Paragraph Discipline

Each paragraph should do one job only.

Typical roles:

- establish a broader pressure or motivation
- narrow to the target task
- identify a bottleneck
- justify a sensing route
- justify a modeling route
- close and hand off to the next section

If one paragraph is simultaneously doing background, review, method, and significance, split or rewrite it.

## Anti-AI-Prose Rules

Watch for:

- overly even paragraph weight
- abstract nouns piled together
- defensive constructions used too often
- generic summary tone without evidence

Typical phrases to reduce:

- “并不是……而是……”
- “不只是……更是……”
- “这说明……”
- “由此可见……”

Use them rarely, not as structural crutches.

## Terminology Compression

Replace wide or floating nouns with the concrete object, property, or use condition they actually refer to.

Examples:

- `区域可比性` -> `支持跨区域对比分析`
- `空间格局` -> `空间分布` or `实际种植分布`
- `可用性` -> `边界位置` / `地块内部一致性` / `地块尺度结果表达`
- `基础数据产品` -> `作物空间数据` or `作物空间分布结果`
- `结果表达` -> rewrite as the concrete map property under discussion

## Citation Matching

Do not attach a pile of papers to every sentence.

Instead:

1. assign each key claim to the smallest authoritative citation set that actually supports it
2. prefer one or two representative papers over a mixed long list
3. use Chinese authoritative reviews for thesis-level Chinese framing when appropriate
4. use method papers only for method-specific claims

If a word in the sentence is not really supported by the cited paper, weaken or rewrite that word.

## Revision Test

Before finalizing a chapter, ask:

1. does this paragraph read like thesis prose or like a review note
2. does the first sentence state the paragraph's real job
3. does each key claim have the right evidence
4. are there any wide nouns that can be replaced with more precise ones
5. does the paragraph naturally lead to the next one
