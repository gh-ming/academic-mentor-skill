# Paper-First Distillation

Use this reference when strengthening the three advisors beyond surface tone.

## Core Claim

For academic mentors, papers are the primary public artifact of judgment.

Compared with interviews or talks, papers expose a more stable combination of:

- what problems the advisor repeatedly chooses
- what scope they consider defensible
- how they define contribution boundaries
- what evidence they consider strong enough
- how they handle uncertainty, limits, and overclaim risk
- how they sequence significance, mechanism, and validation in writing

Therefore:

- papers should outrank interviews for mentor distillation
- papers should usually outrank even talks for logic extraction
- talks and videos are best used to refine explanation rhythm, not to replace paper-grounded judgment

## What To Distill From Papers

For each advisor, inspect papers through five lenses:

### 1. Problem Framing DNA

Extract:

- what bottleneck is named as central
- how broad or narrow the problem statement is
- whether the paper starts from field significance, technical contradiction, or executable need
- whether the paper problem is thesis-worthy, paper-worthy, or method-local

### 2. Contribution Boundary DNA

Extract:

- how aggressively or conservatively claims are stated
- how many contributions are bundled together
- whether the contribution is framed as mechanism, system, benchmark, representation, or workflow
- how the paper avoids promising more than it proves

### 3. Evidence DNA

Extract:

- what experiments are treated as mandatory
- how ablations are used to isolate mechanism
- whether the paper prefers broad generalization evidence or narrow mechanism proof
- how limitations are acknowledged or controlled

### 4. Analysis DNA

Extract:

- whether the paper explains why the method works or only reports gains
- how failure cases, tradeoffs, and uncertainty are treated
- how tightly discussion stays attached to evidence
- whether discussion expands to broader implications or stays technical

### 5. Writing Structure DNA

Extract:

- how introductions move from motivation to gap to contribution
- how methods are decomposed
- how experiments are ordered
- how conclusions compress the work

## Promotion Rules

Promote a paper-derived pattern into mentor rules only when:

- it appears across multiple papers or project artifacts
- it predicts future judgment better than surface tone does
- it remains stable across topic changes

Do not promote:

- one-off stylistic quirks
- venue-specific formatting habits
- citation style or generic section templates

## How This Changes Mentor Behavior

Paper-derived patterns should affect:

- `problem_worldview`
- `method_worldview`
- `evidence_worldview`
- `guidance_style`
- `expression_dna`
- `derived_rules`

But the emphasis should be:

1. paper logic and analysis style
2. then public teaching rhythm
3. then surface expression differences

## Practical Rule

If an interview suggests one style but the paper record suggests another:

- trust the paper record for judgment rules
- trust the talk/video record only for delivery refinement

In short:

papers define what kind of mentor they are;
talks refine how that mentor sounds.
