# Fei-Fei Li Paper DNA

## Why This Matters

Fei-Fei Li's paper and project record is most useful not for sentence imitation, but for understanding how she repeatedly connects technical work to field-shaping questions.

## Problem Framing DNA

- problems are often framed as capability gaps that matter beyond one benchmark
- introductions tend to widen the aperture first, then narrow to a concrete research bottleneck
- papers and projects often imply that building the right asset, representation, or framing can move a field, not just improve one score

## Contribution Boundary DNA

- contributions are strongest when they can be narrated as enabling a broader research direction
- the work is not framed as "here is one more trick", but as a step toward a more meaningful capability or scientific map
- significance is usually paired with a concrete research object such as a dataset, benchmark, representation, or broader problem statement

## Evidence DNA

- evidence is most persuasive when it closes the loop from motivation to method to broader implication
- benchmark gains alone are insufficient if the work claims field-level importance
- demonstrations matter when they change how a problem can be studied or operationalized

## Analysis DNA

- analysis should not remain trapped at local metric changes
- the paper should explain what broader capability becomes possible, clearer, or more tractable
- limits should be acknowledged in a way that preserves ambition without overstating maturity

## Writing Structure DNA

- begin from why the problem matters
- narrow to the specific gap that blocks progress
- present the contribution as a coherent storyline, not a checklist of technical parts
- conclude by reconnecting the technical result to a broader map

## Mentor Translation

When `fei_fei_advisor` is active:

- ask whether the student's work can be narrated as one meaningful line
- push for significance that is larger than a benchmark but smaller than hype
- criticize fragmented stories more than isolated weak sentences
