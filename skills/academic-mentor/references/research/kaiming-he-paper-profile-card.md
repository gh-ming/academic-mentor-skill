# Kaiming He Paper Profile Card

Use this card when the mentor needs paper-level signals from Kaiming He's research writing and contribution discipline.

## Focus

This profile is most useful for:

- clean problem diagnosis
- contribution boundary control
- ablation and mechanism checks
- deciding whether a paper solves a real bottleneck or just assembles complexity

## Introduction Pattern

Recurring pattern:

- move quickly to the central contradiction
- avoid decorative framing when the technical bottleneck itself is the point
- define the problem tightly enough that the reader can test the claim mentally

What this implies for mentor behavior:

- ask the student to state the paper's core problem in one clean sentence
- reject introductions that stay broad because the actual bottleneck is unclear
- punish papers whose framing is vague enough to hide what is truly being solved

## Contribution Pattern

Recurring pattern:

- contributions are strongest when one essential mechanism stays visible
- the paper usually avoids bloated contribution lists
- scope is controlled so that the key insight is not buried under the pipeline

What this implies for mentor behavior:

- ask whether the paper has one real idea or several stitched components
- challenge every component that does not clearly serve the core mechanism
- prefer fewer, cleaner claims over crowded novelty packaging

## Experiment Pattern

Recurring pattern:

- experiments must isolate necessity, not merely show a win
- strong simpler baselines matter because they test whether the added mechanism is justified
- ablations should answer "what is essential?" rather than "what else did we try?"

What this implies for mentor behavior:

- ask whether the current table proves the claimed mechanism or only reports gains
- require controls against simpler alternatives
- cut any experimental branch that obscures attribution

## Limitation Pattern

Recurring pattern:

- it is better to narrow a claim than to keep a vague broad one
- if the mechanism is not isolated, the claim should shrink

What this implies for mentor behavior:

- tell the student to weaken claims before polishing prose
- treat unclear attribution as a first-order problem, not a writing issue

## Diagnostic Questions

- What is the real bottleneck this paper solves?
- Would a much simpler method solve most of the same need?
- Which experiment actually isolates the claimed contribution?
- If the contribution disappears under simpler controls, what remains?
