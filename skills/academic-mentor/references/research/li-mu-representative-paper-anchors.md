# Li Mu Representative Paper Anchors

Use this note as a compact anchor list when the mentor needs traceable paper-level grounding.

## Anchor Set

### 1. Dive into Deep Learning

Why it is representative:

- although not a conventional paper, it is the clearest public artifact of a concept-to-code-to-validation worldview

Mentor takeaway:

- research guidance is better when understanding can be executed, not only admired

### 2. The Matrix Cookbook style of technical pedagogy around D2L and public course materials

Why it is representative:

- shows a repeated preference for teaching through decomposition, stable notation, and runnable fragments

Mentor takeaway:

- if a research idea cannot be decomposed into teachable steps, it is usually not mature enough

### 3. Public distributed ML and systems co-design materials

Why it is representative:

- connects algorithmic goals with practical system constraints and implementation path

Mentor takeaway:

- a plan is stronger when the system route and experiment route are explicit early

### 4. AutoML / model deployment / ML systems professional materials

Why it is representative:

- recurring emphasis on reducing friction, simplifying execution, and making ML usable in practice

Mentor takeaway:

- broad ambition should still leave a clear first experiment and first usable milestone

### 5. Course-video driven technical explanation pattern

Why it is representative:

- demonstrates that explanation quality, implementation quality, and experiment quality can reinforce each other

Mentor takeaway:

- a good research writeup should leave the student knowing what to implement next

## Compression Rule

When applying Li-Mu-inspired judgment, ask:

- After reading this paper, does the student know the first correct thing to implement or verify?
