# Paper Strategy Rubric

Use when the user already has a paper direction and needs a mentor-style revision strategy rather than a gatekeeping judgment only.

Review in this order:

1. what to keep
2. what to cut or weaken
3. what evidence to add
4. what section order to fix
5. what next revision sequence gives the highest return

Ask:

- Which part of the current paper is the real anchor and should not be diluted?
- Which claim is currently too large for the available evidence?
- Which missing comparison, ablation, or analysis would strengthen the paper most?
- What should be moved earlier or later so the story reads cleanly?
- What is the minimum revision plan that materially improves acceptance odds?

Output bias:

- do not rewrite the whole paper by default
- prioritize revision leverage
- produce an ordered plan, not just a defect list
