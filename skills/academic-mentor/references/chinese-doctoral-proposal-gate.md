# Chinese Doctoral Proposal Gate

Use this reference when judging a Chinese doctoral opening report or proposal chapter.

## What To Check

Judge not only factual correctness, but whether the document reads like a real degree-thesis text.

Hard checks:

1. does the section move on one problem-driven line
2. does each paragraph do one job only
3. are important claims supported by the right citations
4. do citations support the prose instead of interrupting it
5. does the surface sound like thesis prose rather than a literature note or assistant draft

## Background And Significance Gate

For `选题背景及意义`, require:

1. a clear chain from larger problem to spatial-information need
2. a clear explanation of why the chosen task is the key data-producing link
3. a justified transition from traditional means to the proposed route
4. no dominant “某某指出/表明” review-style surface
5. no policy-list or application-list drift

If the section spreads its emphasis evenly, explains itself too much, or sounds like a mini related-work section, send it back.

## Anti-AI-Prose Gate

Reject or request revision when you see:

- overly even paragraph weight
- abstract nouns stacked without concrete support
- defensive phrasing used habitually
- generic polished prose with weak evidence
- terminology that sounds formal but does not name a concrete object or property

## Terminology Gate

Pressure-test wide terms such as:

- `区域可比性`
- `空间格局`
- `可用性`
- `基础数据产品`
- `结果表达`

Ask what concrete property the term refers to. If it can be replaced by a narrower object, condition, or evaluation dimension, require that rewrite.

## Citation Gate

Do not reward citation pile-ups.

Check:

1. whether each sentence's key wording is actually supported by the cited paper
2. whether one or two authoritative citations would be better than a long mixed list
3. whether Chinese authoritative reviews are more suitable for Chinese thesis framing
4. whether the paragraph slips into mini related work because of citation phrasing
